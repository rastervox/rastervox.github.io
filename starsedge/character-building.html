Building a Character

When starting play of Starfinder, just as with any TTRPG, the first thing you want to do is make a character. You guys are professionals, you know this, so I won't bore you with the lengthy preamble both the book and the archive does.


Making a character in Starfinder is a relatively simple process.

  1. Make a Concept
  2. Choose a Race (species)
  3. Choose a Theme (background)
  4. Choose a Class
  5. Decide and Apply your Ability Scores
  6. Throw all these into a sheet (the bulk of the work)
I'll try to make a simplified section for each of these steps, to make this as quick and easy as possible. So lets start with concept. I also try to link to the Archives of Nethys, basically the SRD for Starfinder (And both pathfinder versions).

Make a Concept

This is mostly up to the player, and this step is skippable as a first step if you like, as you may have more ideas when looking at the species, themes, and classes of Starfinder. Especially seeing as though I brought over and fleshed out some non-standard species to fill out the galaxy with some more alien-like aliens.


How and why do you want to explore the stars? There is a lot out there, and since you decided to explore, find a reason to be out here, and not just working on mining colony in the middle of nowhere. Running from an oppressive government? Tired of living a sedentary life? Just want to blow some stuff up? They are all good reasons to find solace in space-travel and exploration. Try to find your own.

Choose a Race (Species)

Everyone is born somewhere, and from someone. What exactly are you?


Every species gets an ability score modifier, HP to start with, special features, and other things such as size, speed, and languages.


All pages have more details about lore and such, as well as roleplay notes to conder how a species might act. They have different biology after all.



Choose a Theme (Background)

While technically not the same as a D&D background, they are so similar as to be interchangeable here. A theme is just a core tenet of their backstory, motivations, and previous profession.


If none fit you, and you don't want to use "Themeless", there are others on AON, but I cannot guarentee I will accept them, on the basis of lore conflicts.


Choose a Class

The real meat of a player's abilities, except not really. With how much the other aspects of a character affect how they interface with the world, the class is only one of many parts of a character.


Most of the lore behind these fit the world just as well as in original Starfinder, because while Star's Edge is more grounded, magic is still a thing. It's just less prominent, or has been replaced with technology in some aspect.
